| 1 | [components/LeadCapturePopup.tsx:43](components/LeadCapturePopup.tsx) | The dialog closed itself on navigating into `/admin` or `/checkout` by calling `setOpen(false)` synchronously inside a `useEffect` body тАФ flagged by `react-hooks/set-state-in-effect` (a real correctness/perf smell: calling setState directly in an effect body causes an extra cascading render, rather than reacting to an external system). | ЁЯЯб Medium (perf/correctness, not user-visible today) | ЁЯЯв **Fixed & deployed** |
| 2 | [eslint.config.mjs](eslint.config.mjs) | `globalIgnores` excluded `.next/**`/`out/**`/`build/**`/`next-env.d.ts` but not the two standalone 3D-world side-projects (`sirini-world/`, `sfj-world/` тАФ vendored, minified `three.js` bundles, not part of the Next.js app) or `scripts/` (one-off Node CJS maintenance scripts, already excluded from `tsconfig.json`'s type-checking). Running `npx eslint .` produced **57 false-positive errors** (56 `@typescript-eslint/no-this-alias` + 1 `react-hooks/rules-of-hooks` from minified vendor code, plus 8 `@typescript-eslint/no-require-imports` from the CJS scripts) тАФ noise that would mask a real new lint error in actual app code on any future full-repo lint pass. | ЁЯЯб Medium (tooling hygiene, no runtime impact) | ЁЯЯв **Fixed & deployed** |

**Fix for #1:** moved the dialog-close-on-navigation logic out of the `useEffect` and into React's recommended "adjust state during render" pattern тАФ a `useRef` tracks the previous `isExcludedPage` value, and `setOpen(false)` is called directly in the render body (not inside an effect) only when that value actually changes and the dialog was open. Behaviorally identical (same timing, same result), just avoids the extra render pass the linter was flagging.

**Fix for #2:** added `sirini-world/**`, `sfj-world/**`, and `scripts/**` to `eslint.config.mjs`'s `globalIgnores`.
